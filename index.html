Data mining

Your second point is that you need someone very adept at data mining. That's half of a GIS practitioners existence, I would guess. Or at the very least, it's a very sharp arrow in their quiver.

And as far as what type of role I could fill aiding the journalist in his/her story, I would say I'm capable of both. I can create something based off of a request and I can come up with something myself. GIS practitioners or cartographers have to know how to explain visually. And if I had to, though I have no training as a journalist, I could string together a pretty eloquent narrative.

The following are some web maps I've done outside of work, on my own machine and with open-source resources.



U.S. Bases

This was done with JavaScript. Finding info on military installations can be a bit dubious. I based this off a map I found on Quora.

Wilmington Zoning

This was done with open-source GIS. I created this map for the former building inspector of Wilmington, IL. The parcels and zoning data come from Will County GIS.

Early Illinois Landcover

This was done with JavaScript and open-source GIS. The data came from U. of Illinois Geospatial Data Clearinghouse. Zoom way in and you'll see aerial images beneath the 200 year old landcover data-- which was digitized from the original surveys of the area.
